# Research Vision: Audience, Landscape, and Strategic Directions

*Consolidated from early landscape research and strategic direction analysis.*

## Project Origin

This project aims to build the definitive empirical account of how China's governance system navigates the AI and technological revolution — from central directives to local implementation, from state policy to private sector response, from industrial ambition to social consequences.

## Competitive Landscape

### Databases of Chinese Government Documents

- **China Horizons (Copenhagen Business School / MERICS)** — closest comparable. Central-level policy documents (State Council, Central Committee), 2018-2025. Central-only, no sub-national coverage.
- **DigiChina (Stanford)** — translates and analyzes Chinese tech policy documents. High quality but narrow (digital governance, AI, data privacy).
- **ChinaLawTranslate.com** — crowdsourced translations of legal texts. Law only.
- **ChinaFile's "State of Surveillance"** — scraped ~76,000 government procurement notices for quantitative analysis of surveillance spending. Model of "systematic + humanistic" synthesis.
- **Peking University's Chinalawinfo (北大法宝)** — most comprehensive Chinese legal database. Expensive, Chinese-language, oriented toward legal professionals.
- **Trivium China** — commercial firm parsing local government data for Western investors. Closest commercial analog.

### Key Observation

MERICS documented that online availability of key Chinese government information is declining. Policy transparency peaked ~2015 and has fallen systematically. This makes archiving and contextualizing what IS published increasingly valuable.

## Potential User Groups

| User Group | Key Requirement | Value Proposition |
|---|---|---|
| Policy professionals / think tanks | Track real priorities beyond rhetoric | Curated, searchable, multi-level policy database |
| Political scientists / grad students | Systematic cross-regional comparison | Infrastructure for quantitative governance research |
| MNCs operating in China | Regulatory compliance | Predict local regulations before they become hard law |
| Investors / hedge funds | Track government capital allocation | Provincial government guidance funds, industry bets |
| General educated audience | Understand how China actually works | Humanize governance through local-level documents |

## Four Strategic Directions

### Direction 1: "EIU for Chinese Domestic Policy"
Curated, opinionated interpretation of the document flow. What's signal vs. noise, what's a real policy shift, how does a provincial directive relate to central policy.

### Direction 2: "Vertical-to-Horizontal Translator" (recommended primary focus)
Make the multi-level structure of Chinese governance legible. Show connections between layers (central → provincial → municipal → district) and variation across regions. When the State Council issues a directive, how does implementation differ in Guangdong vs. Gansu?

### Direction 3: "Humanizing China"
Government documents as entry points for human stories. A county health department announcement becomes a lens into rural healthcare access.

### Direction 4: "Policy Evaluation Infrastructure" (recommended long-term)
Structured, searchable, tagged database that researchers can query — like WRDS or FRED but for Chinese government activity. The Yang et al. paper's data collection phase collapses from years to weeks.

### Recommended approach
Start with Direction 2 as content, Direction 4 as long-term structure. Pick policy domains, build vertical chains, write them up as proof of concept.
